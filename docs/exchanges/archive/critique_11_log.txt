## Key Findings

**What's working well:**
- Condition D (fair efficiency comparison) resolves the prior blocking issue — 79.8% token savings at 100% quality retention
- k=3 stability is excellent: σ=0.000 across 4 runs, 97.1% A/C
- The `process_chunk()` library code is sound — monotone merge, no-op detection, retraction accounting all correct
- Paper tables are now comprehensive with 5-run means

**Top priorities I flagged:**

1. **Condition D replication** ($0.06, 30 min) — the headline 79.8% savings is from a single run, and Turn 2 shows a suspicious anomaly (2,052 tokens, 1 iteration for a 2-chunk replay). This must be verified before the paper can claim this number.

2. **Cross-task Condition D** ($0.12) — run on Tasks 3/6 to confirm efficiency generalizes

3. **Dynamic context proof-of-concept** ($1-2, 2 hrs) — after 15 iterations, zero experiments test the thesis motivation (dynamic context). Even a minimal 3-turn experiment with genuine entity attribute changes would strengthen the paper's framing.

**Code issues:** `process_chunk()` mutates caller's dicts during monotone merge (needs documentation), no test coverage for the Condition D code generator (which already had 2 regex bugs), and a cherry-picking risk in paper tables showing "best run" alongside means.
