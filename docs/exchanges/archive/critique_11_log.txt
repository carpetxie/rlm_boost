## Critique Summary — Iteration 11

**Two previously unidentified structural issues** were found by inspecting `label_aware_task1_results.json` against the actual chunking code:

### Critical Finding 1: Coverage Non-Equivalence in A/C Comparison
`split_context_by_users()` applied to the 96K labeled corpus produces 5 disjoint ~5K windows spread across the full corpus — not 5 sequential windows from the same first 25K chars that oracle C sees. Condition A covers all 231 users shallowly (breadth-first); Condition C covers ~60 users deeply (depth-first). These are **different data slices with different coverage ceilings**. The "49.5% of oracle" (F1=0.1695 vs 0.3424) headline is comparing apples to oranges and cannot be published as-is. **Fix**: 10 lines of code — split `labeled_context[:25000]` into 5 sequential 5K windows.

### Critical Finding 2: Turn 1 Phantom Chunk
`chunks_processed` jumped to 2 after Turn 1 (given only chunk 0's context) — the model called `process_chunk(1, {empty_entities}, ...)` alongside chunk 0 in consecutive REPL iterations. This permanently polluted chunk 1 in the deduplication guard with zero entities, causing Turn 2 to be blocked (non-compliant). The true compliance rate is 60%, not 80%. **Fix**: Add "Call process_chunk EXACTLY ONCE with chunk_idx={chunk_idx}" to the prompt template.

### What Holds Independently
- **P=1.0 (zero false positives)** across all conditions and turns survives any experiment redesign — it's a property of the label-aware checker, not the data slice
- **REPL-state as correctness ground truth** is a clean architectural insight, independently valid
- **Retraction taxonomy** (360× range, temporal asymmetry mechanism) is empirically novel

### The One Big Thing
Redesign Condition A to use sequential 5K windows from `labeled_context[:25000]` — same data as oracle C. Expected: F1 improves substantially from 0.1695; P=1.0 maintained; A/C comparison becomes internally valid.
