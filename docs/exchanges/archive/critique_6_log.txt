### Three New Code/Architecture Issues

1. **O(u·n) complexity gap** — the "updated × all" loop in `process_chunk()` is O(u × n) per chunk, not O(k·n). At N=10K entities with 10% update rate, this produces 10M pair checks per chunk — worse than full recompute. The scalability claim must be corrected to O((k+u)·n).

2. **Role-ordering defect in `_prune_with_summary`** — the pruned history produces consecutive `(user, user)` and `(assistant, assistant)` message pairs at the seam, making the "PRIOR COMPUTATION SUMMARY" appear as user input rather than prior model output. Fix: swap `summary_message` to `"assistant"` role and `ack_message` to `"user"`.

3. **σ model sign inconsistency** — `model_sigma_param` uses `c*σ*(1-d/k)` with d=-1.60, producing `(1+1.60/k)`. Reparameterize to `(1+e/k)` with e>0 to eliminate sign ambiguity in the paper formula.

### The One Big Thing
**Run the live API experiment and use the compliance rate to lock in the paper's contribution framing.** After six iterations, either: (A) compliance ≥50% → empirical paper; or (B) compliance <50% → theoretical architecture paper that motivates fine-tuning as a prerequisite. Both are publishable. The wrong path is maintaining the "empirical system" framing without the data.
